01 — About

Who's
behind this.

I am Ajit Kumar. I spent fifteen years inside India's public sector banking system. I have an MA in Economics that I almost forgot. This is the channel I needed when I was starting out — and didn't have.

Most economics teaching is too theoretical. Most banking talk is too sales-driven. The truth lives in between.

For a decade and a half, I sat across from people taking home loans, MSMEs raising working capital, retirees rebalancing fixed deposits. I watched RBI rate decisions ripple through people's monthly EMIs. I watched fiscal policy meet the kitchen budget.

Textbooks describe these chains. But they don't make them feel real. That gap — between what is written and what is lived — is what SaralArthaShastra exists to close.

I teach Economics the way I now wish I had been taught it. With real examples. With Indian numbers. With the honest admission that the economy is messier than equations suggest.

04 — Philosophy

Four rules.
That's all.

A short list that governs every video, course, and email sent under this name. If something violates any of these — it doesn't ship.

01

Plain language

If a 16-year-old can't follow it, the explanation is wrong. Jargon is laziness disguised as expertise.

02

Real numbers

Indian data, current quarter. No outdated examples from a 2008 textbook. Living economics, not preserved ones.

03

Honest uncertainty

When the data is contested, you'll hear both sides. When I'm not sure, I'll say so. Confidence isn't a substitute for truth.

04

No hype

No "secret tricks." No "rich in 30 days." No fear-mongering thumbnails. Education over engagement metrics.
